About Me

In regards to technology, I guess you can say that I have always been a little bit too optimistic. This was particularly evident at the age of 13 when I was unshakeably convinced that I could build a working satellite with nothing more than some junk from the recycling center, a raspberry pi, and a patient Step Dad with some DIY experience. Unfortunately, I was unable to convince NASA to help me launch my satellite, however, my desire to build great things with technology has always stuck with me.

I have been programming since 2013, which is the year I came across TheNewBoston's Python tutorials on YouTube. Despite not being the greatest set of Python tutorials available, they were what gave me a rudimentary understanding of many basic programming concepts. Since then, I have gained experience with a wide range of programming languages, including Python, Java, Kotlin, C++, C, Haskell, and more. Just a few of the projects in which I made use of these languages can be found here.

In July 2020, I graduated from Swansea University with a BSc (Hons) in Software Engineering. Next year, I plan on completing an MSc by Research course in Computer Science, so that I can further develop my undergraduate dissertation project related to Natural Language Processing / Text Simplification. Alongside programming, I enjoy travelling, reading, scrabble, and Reese's peanut butter cups. I also like to run, however, the peanut butter cups tend to negate any progress. Oh well. ¯\_(ツ)_/¯
